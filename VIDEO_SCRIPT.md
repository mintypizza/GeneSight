# GeneSight Video Script — 3 Minute Demo
# ========================================
# Read this while screen-recording the app in action.
# Aim for a calm, confident, presenter tone.
# Total time: ~2:50

---

## [0:00 – 0:25] THE HOOK — Show the Problem

> "Every year, millions of people receive genetic test results they can't understand. Worldwide, over 300 million people are affected by rare diseases — and on average, it takes four to seven years to get a correct diagnosis. That's years of uncertainty, misdiagnosis, and suffering — what clinicians call the 'diagnostic odyssey.'

> The problem isn't just the science. It's access. There are fewer than 5,000 clinical geneticists in the United States, and in much of the developing world, there are almost none. And because 95% of genomic reference data comes from European populations, underserved communities are hit the hardest."

**[ON SCREEN: Show the GeneSight app landing page]**

---

## [0:25 – 0:55] THE SOLUTION — Introduce GeneSight

> "That's why we built GeneSight — an AI-powered genetic variant interpreter, powered by Google's Gemma 4.

> GeneSight acts as an autonomous genetic counselor. You give it a variant — in any format — and it does what a trained geneticist would do: it queries clinical databases, reads the scientific literature, applies classification guidelines, checks for drug interactions, and synthesizes everything into a clear, evidence-based report.

> And here's the key: your genetic data never leaves your device. GeneSight is designed privacy-first. Only gene names and database IDs are sent to public scientific APIs. The analysis runs locally through Gemma 4."

**[ON SCREEN: Point to the privacy banner, show the sidebar configuration]**

---

## [0:55 – 1:50] THE DEMO — Show It Working

> "Let me show you how it works. I'll analyze BRCA1 c.68_69del — one of the most well-known pathogenic variants, a founder mutation linked to hereditary breast and ovarian cancer."

**[ON SCREEN: Click the BRCA1 demo button, then click "Analyze with Gemma 4"]**

> "Watch what happens. Gemma 4 receives my query and starts reasoning. It decides which tools to call — first ClinVar for clinical significance, then UniProt for protein function, PubMed for the latest research, and the ACMG assessment engine for systematic classification.

> Each step is visible in the interface — you can see exactly what the AI is doing and why. This is transparency by design."

**[ON SCREEN: Show the tool calls appearing in real-time, the progress bar moving]**

> "And here are the results. The dashboard shows the pathogenicity gauge, the ACMG evidence radar, and a gene-disease-drug network graph — all generated from live database queries. Every claim is backed by a citation."

**[ON SCREEN: Switch to the Results Dashboard tab, scroll through the visualizations]**

---

## [1:50 – 2:25] THE REPORTS — Show the Output

> "GeneSight generates two types of reports. The clinical report is detailed and technical — designed for healthcare providers. It includes the full evidence trail, every database that was queried, and ACMG criteria scoring.

> But we also generate a patient-friendly report — written in plain language that a patient or family member can understand. Because access to genetic knowledge shouldn't require a medical degree."

**[ON SCREEN: Switch to the Reports tab. Show both the clinical report and patient report. Click the download buttons.]**

> "Both reports can be downloaded instantly. And because everything is cached locally, you can re-analyze the same variant offline — critical for clinics with spotty internet."

---

## [2:25 – 2:50] THE IMPACT — Why It Matters

> "GeneSight isn't just a demo. It's a blueprint for how open AI models can democratize specialized medical knowledge.

> With Gemma 4's native function calling, we've built a system that reasons through complex genetic evidence the same way a human expert would — but at a fraction of the cost, available to anyone, anywhere.

> Imagine a community health worker in rural India, a genetic counselor in sub-Saharan Africa, or a family in Appalachia — all with access to the same quality of genetic interpretation as a patient at a top research hospital.

> That's the promise of GeneSight. Expert-level genetic interpretation, powered by Gemma 4 — private, accessible, and grounded in science."

**[ON SCREEN: Show the About tab with architecture diagram, then fade to GeneSight logo]**

> "GeneSight. Built with Gemma 4. Built for everyone."

---

# TIPS FOR RECORDING
- Screen record at 1080p or higher
- Use a quiet room with a good microphone
- Keep the app in dark mode (it's the default)
- Practice reading through once before recording
- Pause naturally between sections — don't rush
- Upload to YouTube as Unlisted or Public
